What is Cyber Threat Intelligence?

Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) or threat intel is detailed, actionable information about cybersecurity threats. Threat intelligence helps security teams take a more proactive approach to detecting, mitigating and preventing cyberattacks.

Threat intelligence refers to the collection, processing, and analysis of data to understand a threat actors motives, targets, and attack methods. It transforms raw data into actionable insights, enabling security teams to make informed, data-driven decisions. This shifts organizations from a reactive to a proactive stance in defending against cyber threats.

Threat intelligence cycle

  • Planning and direction
  • Collection
  • Processing and exploitation
  • Analysis
  • Dissemination
  • Feedback

Cyber intelligence begins with planning and direction, which enables effective preparation for any potential threat. The next step in the cycle includes Collection, which involves gathering relevant intelligence. Then, Processing and exploitation, which might be considered a key component of a threat intelligence platform, as it is a potent tool for analysts.

Next is the analysis, which brings all the collected and processed information together and analyses it for new or key information that can provide context on the facing threats. This then looks for patterns in data. After that, it is Dissemination, which is the phase where the analyst takes all the information they have identified and applies the relevant context for their customer into a finished intelligence report. The finished report can come in many forms. The last stage of the cycle is feedback, which involves gathering feedback from customers.

What are the types of threat intelligence?

  • Stragic threat intelligence
  • Tactical Threat Intelligence
  • Techincal Threat intelligence
  • Operational Threat intelligence

Strategic threat intelligence

Strategic threat intelligence provides the threat landscape in a broad view for organisations It focuses on risks, potential vulnerabilities and the goals of threat actors. This type of intelligence helps guide overall security strategy and a allocation of resource offering insights that are less technical but important for long-term planning and a risk management.

Tactical Threat Intelligence

The tactical threat intelligence offers the specific details about threat actors tactics and techniques and the procedures TTP for the security teams. It helps to identify potential attack vectors and guides development of defense strategies. This intelligence highlights system vulnerabilities and provides the insights on how to detect and mitigate specific types of a attack ultimately strengthening excisting controls.
