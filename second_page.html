What is Cyber Threat Intelligence?

Threat intelligence refers to the collection, processing,
and analysis of data to understand a threat actors motives,
targets, and attack methods.

It transforms raw data into actionable insights, enabling security teams to make informed,
data-driven decisions. This shifts organizations from a reactive to a proactive stance in defending against cyber threats.

The intelligence cycle-

  • Planning and direction
  • Collection
  • Processing and exploitation
  • Analysis
  • Dissemination
  • Feedback

    • Cyber intelligence begins with planning and direction,
    which enables effective preparation for any potential threat. The next step in the cycle includes Collection,
    which involves gathering relevant intelligence. Then, Processing and exploitation, which might be considered a key component of a threat intelligence platform,
    as it is a potent tool for analysts.

    Next is the analysis, which brings all the collected and processed information together and analyses it for new or key information that can provide context on the facing threats.

    This then looks for patterns in data. After that, it is Dissemination,
    which is the phase where the analyst takes all the information they have identified and applies the relevant context for their customer into a finished intelligence report. The finished report can come in many forms.
    The last stage of the cycle is feedback, which involves gathering feedback from customers.
